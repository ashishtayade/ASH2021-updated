Pyruvate Kinase Red Blood Cell (PKR) Isoform: Structure and Function1,2

  • PKR is a 240 kDa tetramer
  • Binds to both PEP and FBP
  • Catalyzes the conversion of PEP to pyruvate, which
    • Generates 50% of ATP produced via glycolysis

Mitapivat (AG-348) Activates Wild-Type and Mutant PKR1

  • A. Mitapivat, a small molecule activator, binds at the dimer-dimer interface of the PKR tetramer
  • B. Mitapivat binding enhances the affinity of recombinant PKR for its substrate PEP
  • C. Mitapivat increases activity of recombinant wild-type and mutant PKR enzymes
    • Mitapivat increases PKR catalytic activity in cases in which the mutation results in decreased enzyme activity, e.g. R486W
    • Mitapivat increases PKR stability in cases in which the mutation reduces enzyme stability, e.g. R510Q

PEP = phosphoenolpyruvate
1. Kung et al., Blood, 2017; 130(11):1347-1356.

Mitapivat Increased ATP and Improved RBC Parameters in the Hbbth3/+ Mouse Model of β-thalassemia

Republished with permission of J Clin Invest, from The pyruvate kinase activator mitapivat reduces hemolysis and improves anemia in a β-thalassemia mouse model, Matte A, 131(10) 2021; permission conveyed through Copyright Clearance Center, Inc.
    • Mitapivat ameliorated anemia, increased Hb, and enhanced red blood cell survival after 21 days of oral treatment in the Hbbth3/+ mouse model
    • Mitapivat reduced signs and markers of ineffective erythropoiesis and liver iron overload (data not shown)

*p<0.05 vs vehicle
ATP = adenosine triphosphate; Hbbth3/+ = heterozygote mouse model of β-thalassemia.

Mitapivat Increased PKR Activity and ATP in RBCs from Patients with SCD and β-thalassemia

  • In ex vivo analyses of RBCs from patients with SCD (HbSS; n=26), HbSC (n=4), β-thalassemia major (N=3, regularly transfused) and 21 healthy controls (HC)1:
PK activity was significantly reduced relative to HK in HbSS and β-thalassemia RBCs compared to healthy controls
Mitapivat increased PK activity and ATP levels in HbSS and β-thalassemia RBCs and in healthy controls

****p<0.0001, *** p<0.001, ** p<0.01, ns, not significant,
¥, relative to DMSO control; error bars represent standard deviation
ATP = adenosine triphosphate; PK = pyruvate kinase; HK = hexokinase; HC = healthy controls; HbSC = heterozygous for HbS and HbC;
HbSS = homozygous sickle cell disease; RBC = red blood cell; SCD = sickle cell disease
