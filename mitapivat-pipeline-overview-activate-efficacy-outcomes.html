Efficacy Outcomes

STATUS: CORE STUDY COMPLETED
EXTENSION ONGOING
Primary Endpoint
16/40 (40%) patients randomized to mitapivat achieved a hemoglobin responsea, compared to 0 patients randomized to placebo (2-sided p<0.0001)
Secondary Endpoints
The Hb increase occurred early and was sustained: average change from baseline in Hb for mitapivat was 1.67 g/dL compared with -0.15 g/dL for placebo (2-sided p<0.0001) at Weeks 16, 20, and 24
Statistically significant improvements compared to placebo were demonstrated for the PK deficiency PRO measures:
  • Pyruvate Kinase Deficiency Diary (PKDD), a daily diary of signs and symptoms
  • Pyruvate Kinase Deficiency Impact Assessment (PKDIA), a weekly measure of disease impacts, measures of health-related QoL
STUDY DESIGN SAFETY

aHemoglobin response is defined as a > increase in hemoglobin concentration from baseline that is sustained at two or more scheduled assessments at Weeks 16, 20, and 24 during the optimized-dose portion of the trial.
