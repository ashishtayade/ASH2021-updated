STATUS: CORE STUDY COMPLETED
EXTENSION ONGOING
All patients (N = 52) experienced at least 1 AE, the majority of which were grade 1 or 2 in severity

As of 31 August 2018, the most common AEs were transient and generally resolved within 7 days1
18 serious AEs were reported in 15 patients, all as single events with the exception of pharyngitis and hemolytic anemia (n = 2 each)1
As of 27 March 2019, AEs for patients who continued in the study (n = 18) were comparable in the core and extension periods2
    • In the extension period, the most common AEs were headache (39%), insomnia (28%), fatigue (28%), and nasopharyngitis (28%)
    • Chronic daily dosing within mitapivat for a median of 3 years and up to 42 months was well tolerated
    • Consistent safety profile over the duration of treatment
    • No new safety signals were indentified in the extension period
